# Task Prompt

A FieldTrace Rescue & Supply Chain management system that includes offline account access, evidence capture, standardized item data, traceability publishing controls, controlled facility check-ins, and analytics for day-to-day shelter and warehouse operations. Staff users sign in with a local username and password on a Leptos web UI and are assigned roles of Administrator, Operations Staff, and Auditor. The home workspace shows today’s intake and transfer queue, pending inspections, and exceptions; actions give immediate in-app feedback such as “saved locally,” “needs review,” or “blocked by policy.” Users maintain their profile, privacy preferences, and a local address book for shipping destinations (US format with ZIP+4 validation and phone masking), and can request account deletion with a 7-day cooling-off period before irreversible removal. For media and evidence, Operations Staff can capture photos, short videos (up to 60 seconds), or audio notes (up to 2 minutes) directly from the device; the UI automatically stamps a visible watermark containing facility code and local timestamp (MM/DD/YYYY hh:mm AM/PM) and flags files missing EXIF capture time when available. Evidence can be linked to an intake record, inspection result, traceability code, or check-in event, then searched by keyword, tag, or capture date, with default retention of 365 days and manual legal-hold override. Product and supply entries support guided parsing for item name, SKU, size, color, price and discount in USD, stock status, media references, and short review summaries from staff notes, then present a standardized view with unit conversion (oz/lb, in/ft) and consistent naming to reduce duplicates. The check-in ledger supports scanning a barcode or manual entry of a member ID; facial-recognition is shown only as a placeholder toggle with no biometric processing, and anti-passback prevents re-entry within 2 minutes unless an Administrator overrides with a reason. A dashboard provides filters by region, time range, status, and tags, plus full-text search and CSV export for metrics such as rescue volume, adoption conversion, task completion rate, donations logged offline, and inventory on hand.

The backend uses Axum to expose local REST-style APIs consumed by the Leptos frontend, with SQLite as the single-node persistence layer so the entire system remains fully functional without internet access or third-party services. All writes are validated server-side: passwords must be at least 12 characters with a stored salted hash; sessions expire after 30 minutes of inactivity; role-based access blocks publishing or retracting traceability information to Administrators and Auditors only, and every publish/retract requires a mandatory comment and is versioned for audit. Structured parsing applies deterministic rules: enum mapping normalizes colors (e.g., “navy” to “blue”), sizes are converted to a canonical unit, missing values are completed only from local defaults (never inferred from external sources), and conflicts trigger a “needs review” state. Media ingestion performs format and fingerprint validation, enforces maximum file sizes (25 MB photo, 150 MB video, 20 MB audio), compresses locally, and supports resumable uploads in 2 MB chunks; evidence links are immutable once referenced by a traceability record. Traceability codes are generated locally with a checksum and can be verified offline; records aggregate process steps and inspection outcomes, and retraction immediately hides public-facing views while preserving the audit trail. Observability is built in end-to-end: each ingestion, parsing, and publish job emits structured logs, basic metrics, and trace identifiers stored locally; operators can view job run reports, see failure root-cause notes, roll back configuration to any of the last 10 saved versions, and export a one-click diagnostic package (logs, metrics snapshot, config history) as a ZIP file. Sensitive fields such as phone numbers and addresses are encrypted at rest with a locally managed key, and on-screen displays mask all but the last four digits to minimize incidental exposure.
